# CS250_Portfolio-
Reflection
1. How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?

I interpret user needs by focusing on what the user is trying to accomplish and why it matters. Breaking these needs down into smaller, clear requirements helps make them easier to implement in a program. Creating user stories is especially helpful because they put the focus on the user’s perspective. They help guide development by clearly defining what the system should do and what success looks like. This makes it easier to prioritize features and ensure the final product provides real value.

2. How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?

I approach developing programs by breaking work into smaller, manageable pieces and focusing on continuous improvement. Throughout this course, I learned the importance of iterative development, regular feedback, and adapting to change. In the future, I plan to incorporate Agile practices such as sprint planning, daily stand-ups, and retrospectives. These processes help keep work organized, improve communication, and allow for faster adjustments when requirements change.

3. What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?

Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being reliable, and supporting others on the team. It also involves being open to feedback and willing to adapt when needed. A strong team member contributes to a positive team environment by collaborating effectively and helping solve problems. I’ve learned that teamwork is just as important as technical skills when it comes to successfully completing a project.
